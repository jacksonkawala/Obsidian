# Freeboard

The vertical distance between the water (pool) surface and the top of the dam crest. Freeboard is a critical safety parameter for tailings storage facilities — insufficient freeboard risks overtopping, which can lead to catastrophic dam failure.

## TARP Levels at [[Nataka TSF]]

Freeboard is monitored against a Trigger Action Response Plan (TARP) with color-coded levels:

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Green** | Normal operations |
| **Blue** | Elevated awareness |
| **Yellow** | Action required — increased monitoring |
| **Orange** | Significant concern — escalate |
| **Red** | Critical — emergency response |

As of W15 2026, [[Nataka TSF]] freeboard is at **5.40 m (YELLOW)**, declining from ~7.0 m over recent weeks.

## Relevance to [[Critical State Soil Mechanics]]

Freeboard management connects to CSSM through:
- **Pore pressure dissipation** — tailings consolidation rates affect pool level
- **Effective stress** — rising pool reduces effective stress in the embankment
- **Seepage analysis** — phreatic surface position depends on pool level relative to crest
