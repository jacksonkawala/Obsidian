## Highlights — Critical Alerts

### WCPC Water Body — RED ALERT

Water level at 10.10 mRL, [[Freeboard]] at **0.90 m** (below 1.0 m minimum). **Non-compliant.** Recommended to halt all inflows into the water body.

### Paddock 73 — RED ALERT

Water level at 42.00 mRL, [[Freeboard]] at **1.70 m**. Continue operating Godwin pump at maximum capacity. Continue raising the low section on berm 73.

### Old DM Pit — ORANGE ALERT

Water level at 21.10 mRL, exceeding max allowable limit of 21.00 mRL by 0.10 m. [[Freeboard]] at **1.90 m**. Progressive erosion observed along separation berm between Old DM Pit and Paddock 11C — compromising structural integrity. Diverting MSP rejects to Paddock 9C and running electrical pump in Finishing Pond at max capacity.

### C1 Tails — WCPC

Accumulated tailings have altered the natural flow direction, migrating toward the community access road. This is causing erosion and extending into the valley, posing a **contamination risk**. A minimum 1.0 m freeboard must be maintained during discharge on the port side. The community access road requires reinstatement.

### Exit Pond

Water level at 10.70 mRL (max allowable). Turbidity exceeds standard — **no discharge to environment permitted**. Pump water back to WCPB Pond. Consider raising low sections for increased capacity.

### SMO

Tailings are encroaching on the separation berm and slumps are developing, posing a **risk of berm breach**. A breach could release tailings into the main pond, reducing effective storage capacity. Recurrent stacker washouts are also eroding the sea wall.

**Actions:** Extend finger berm (in progress), expedite new sump excavation, install geofabric on the sea wall, and ensure stackers do not operate at low density.

### HTC

The buildup is visibly exceeding the 1.0 m freeboard from the NGL, posing a **risk of backflow toward Mualadi Lake**. Maintain a minimum 1.0 m freeboard, extend or relocate the HTC promptly, and optimise HTC operating hours.
