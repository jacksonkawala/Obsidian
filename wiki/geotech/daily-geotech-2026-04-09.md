# Daily Geotechnical Report — 09 April 2026

**Ref:** 080/GTC/2026 | **Date:** 09-04-2026 | **Time:** 06:00–11:00 AM

## Highlights

### C1 Tails — WCPC (NEW concern)
The accumulation of C1 tailings remains a significant concern. The buildup could alter flow direction toward the community access road, causing renewed erosion and potentially channelling discharge into the valley with adverse environmental impacts. A minimum 1.00 m [[Freeboard]] must be maintained on the port side during discharge. Continuous monitoring and timely relocation of discharge points are strongly recommended.

### WCPC Water Body — RED ALERT (unchanged)
Water level still at **10.10 mRL**, [[Freeboard]] **0.90 m** (Red Alert). Remains non-compliant. **Halt all inflows.**

### Paddock 73 — IMPROVED from RED to ORANGE
Water level decreased 250 mm over 24 hours: **42.00 → 41.75 mRL**. [[Freeboard]] improved to **1.95 m** (Orange Alert). Godwin pump operating at maximum capacity. Continue raising low section of Berm 73.

### SMO — Significant Concern (escalated)
[[Kenmare Moma Mining|SMO]] tailings management remains a significant concern:
- Tailings encroaching on the separation berm, developing slumps → **berm breach risk**
- Breach would release tailings into main pond, reducing effective storage capacity
- Recurrent stacker washouts eroding the sea wall, spilling water over operational area

**Actions:**
1. Extend the finger berm to safeguard pumps (in progress)
2. Expedite excavation of a new sump
3. Install geofabric on the sea wall
4. Ensure stackers are not running on low density

### HTC — Buildup Concern (ongoing)
The buildup visibly exceeds the 1.0 m [[Freeboard]] from the beach to the NGL, posing a risk of backflow toward Mualadi Lake. Maintain a minimum 1.0 m freeboard, extend or relocate the HTC promptly, and optimise HTC operating hours.
