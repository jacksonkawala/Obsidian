# Daily Geotechnical Report — 13 April 2026

**Ref:** 083/GTC/2026 | **Date:** 13-04-2026 | **Time:** 06:00–11:00

## Highlights

### WCPC Water Body — RED ALERT (unchanged)

Water level has dropped to 9.85 mRL but freeboard remains non-compliant at 1.15 m. The Godwin pump should continue operating and all inflows must remain suspended.

### WCPA Pond Water Level — NEW RED ALERT

The WCPA pond water level rose 300 mm over 24 hours (38.30 to 38.50 mRL), reducing freeboard to 2.70 m and triggering a Red Alert. The agreed response actions are:
- Keep TSF pumps switched off
- Run slimes line to PDP-1 with close monitoring to avoid PDP-1 non-compliance
- Divert MSP Rejects line (from Finishing Pond) to discharge into the RWD

Significant water level drop of 0.45 m, continuing the positive recovery trend. Side berm seepage is present. Ongoing issues:

- **Liner theft continues** — another section of TSF starter wall liner stolen, exposing the wall to erosion and seepage during the rainy season

- **Pipeline hanging** at the starter wall bend — progressive collapse risk into the facility; repositioning and securing recommended

- **3A stacker U/F erosion** on Lift 1 area worsened by rainfall

The 3A stacker washouts have led to wet tailings encroaching on Lift 1 construction, eroding the compacted sidewall and flooding the access road. A field meeting was convened and the following actions agreed:
1. Stop and relocate the stacker (done)
2. Construct retention berms at the new position
3. Maintain proper density (plant currently shut)
4. Remediate affected areas — remove wet tailings, recompact, retest
5. Maintain safe distances between stackers and hauling points
6. Restrict HME movement near operating stackers
