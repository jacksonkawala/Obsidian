# Daily Geotechnical Report — 16 April 2026

**Ref:** 086/GTC/2026 | **Date:** 16-04-2026 | **Time:** 06:00–11:00

## Highlights

### WCPC Water Body — RED ALERT (sixth consecutive day, slight deterioration)
Water level at 9.90 mRL, freeboard **1.10 m** — down 0.05 m from 1.15 m (held for five straight days). Condition remains critical and non-compliant. Godwin pump to continue running; all inflows to remain suspended.

### Tails 3A Stacker — NEW WASHOUT TODAY
A washout occurred at the Tails 3A stacker and operations were halted immediately on discovery. The retention berm at the new (relocated) stacker position is **both narrow and short**, and an access road runs along its base — a configuration that exposes heavy mobile equipment to bogging in liquefied sand tailings. Recommended measures: reinforce the retention berms, maintain proper density, enforce safe separation distances, assign a dedicated spotter, raise team awareness, and restrict road access during stacker operation. This is the second active stacker washout in consecutive days (BB1 on 15 Apr, 3A on 16 Apr).

### MSP Rejects — Flow Reversal in Paddock 16C
MSP Rejects discharged into Paddock 16C have accumulated to the point that the flow direction reversed, causing backflow and sidewall erosion that is compromising structural integrity. Discharge to be reallocated and extended to prevent further risk. Paddock 16C was adopted as the redirected destination for MSP Rejects only yesterday (15 Apr) after Old DM Pit entered Orange Alert — the rejection rate clearly exceeds the paddock's capacity.
