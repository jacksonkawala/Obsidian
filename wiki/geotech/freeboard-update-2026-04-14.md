# Freeboard Update Report — 14 April 2026

**Ref:** 09/WFR/2026 | **Date:** 14-04-2026

Comprehensive section-by-section freeboard assessment based on topographic/drone survey data. This report is fundamentally different from the [[Daily Geotechnical Report — 14 April 2026|daily geotech inspections]] — it provides surveyed cross-sections at multiple points around each facility, identifying the true controlling (minimum) freeboard section and its crest geometry.

## Critical Findings

Seven facilities have sections with freeboard below 2.0 m:

| Facility | Section | Freeboard (m) | Crest Width (m) | Survey Date |
|----------|---------|--------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **WCPC Water Body** | PT_4 | **1.05** | 6.00 | 20-03-2026 |
| **Paddock 10C** | Sec_6 | **1.25** | 7.00 | 26-09-2025 |
| **SMO Pond** | Sec_6 | **1.40** | 16.00 | 24-03-2026 |
| **Starboard Side Corner** | Stb_2 | **1.45** | 3.50 | 26-03-2026 |
| **Paddock 73** (B73_1 at 30m) | B73_1 | **1.50** | 22.00 | survey date n/a |
| **Paddock 9C** | 9C_3 | **1.50** | 2.40 | 26-09-2025 |
| **Exit Pond** | Sec_2 | **1.60** | 2.00 | 26-03-2026 |

Several of these have dangerously narrow crest widths: Exit Pond Sec_2 at only 2.0 m, Paddock 9C 9C_3 at 2.4 m, and Starboard Side Corner Stb_2 at 3.5 m. Narrow crests are vulnerable to erosion, traffic damage, and wave overtopping.

**B73_1 is the critical section.** The crest elevation is 45.00 mRL (3.50 m freeboard), but the berm profile drops to 43.00 mRL at the 30m width measurement point, giving only **1.50 m of freeboard**. With WL at 41.50 and the daily geotech reports showing P73 reversed to 41.60 on 15 Apr, this section would have only **1.40 m** of freeboard at the 30m width, or possibly less.

This is a stacker-built berm — the geometry is irregular and the lower elevation at 30m width indicates the berm flank is not fully built up. The seepage daylighting at the toe of Berm 73 (reported since 10 Apr) is occurring at precisely this vulnerable section.

## Data Quality Notes

1. **Survey dates vary widely** — from 26 Sep 2025 (Paddock 9C, 10C, Finishing Pond) to 10 Apr 2026 (WCPC Pond). The older surveys may not reflect current berm geometry, especially where stacker construction or erosion has occurred.
2. **Water levels are snapshots** — the report uses surveyed WL values that may differ from daily readings. The TSF WL of 23.35 mRL matches the 13 Apr geotech morning reading.
3. **WCPC WL discrepancy** — survey uses 9.95/10.20 mRL vs daily geotech 9.85 mRL. The 10.20 value may be from the 10 Apr survey date.
