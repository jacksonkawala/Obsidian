# Kenmare Moma Mining (Mauritius) Limited

Heavy mineral sands mining operation at Moma, Mozambique. Also operates as Kenmare Moma Processing (Mauritius) Limited.

## Facilities — Water Containment & Processing

- **[[Nataka TSF]]** — Tailings Storage Facility for slimes deposition (Phase 1, Lift 1 in progress). Pool 24.50 mRL on 16 Apr (+0.20 m), freeboard 5.50 m Yellow.
- **WCP-A Dredge Pond** — Active dredge mining pond with 4 mining faces (Calen, Sandra, Port Side, Starboard). WL stable at 38.00 mRL on 15–16 Apr. Freeboard 3.20 m starboard / 4.00 m Berm 75. 110 kV pole erosion requires reinstatement; decanting pipe blockage near staging pond.
- **WCPA** — Water Containment and Processing Area (includes TSF, PDP, WCP-A)
- **WCPC** — Water body at **RED ALERT — sixth consecutive day**. Freeboard dropped to **1.10 m** on 16 Apr (was 1.15 m for five days). Non-compliant. Godwin pump running, inflows suspended. [[Freeboard Update Report — 14 April 2026|Survey confirms]] the controlling section (PT_4) has only **1.05 m freeboard** on a 6 m wide crest — the lowest on the entire site.
- **PDP-1** — Primary Densification Pond 1 (water level 34.30 mRL on 16 Apr, down 0.10 m). Port liner ripped off again.
- **PDP-2** — Primary Densification Pond 2 (water level 31.90 mRL, stable for four consecutive days). Port liner ripped off again; starboard erosion near slimes boosters worsening.
- **Finishing Pond-C** — feeds into WCP-A
- **RAW Dam** — make-up and main line water supply
- **Exit Pond** — discharge point (currently at max allowable, turbidity non-compliant)
- **Float-off Pond** — WL 9.10 mRL (dropping). Erosion at entrance from rainfall; river discharge pipe needs lifting.
- **SMO** — Surface Mining Operations area (berm breach risk — tailings encroaching, slumps developing, sea wall erosion from stacker washouts). Survey confirms Sec_6 at only 1.40 m freeboard.
- **HTC** — buildup risk of backflow toward Mualadi Lake
- **WCP-B Pond** — WL 11.00 mRL (+0.05 m). Mining face and starboard sidewall freeboard both 2.00 m. Portside protection berm not constructed.
- **Old DM Pit** — **NEW ORANGE ALERT** as of 15 Apr — WL rose 500 mm (20.70→21.20 mRL), freeboard 1.80 m. MSP Rejects redirected to Paddock 16C.
- **Exit Pond** — WL 10.10 mRL. Survey shows five of eight sections below 2.0 m freeboard; Sec_2 at only 1.60 m on a 2 m wide crest.
- **Starboard Side Corner** — WL 14.55 mRL. Stb_2 at 1.45 m freeboard on 3.5 m crest — a pinch point.

## Facilities — Paddocks

- **Paddock 73** — Slight improvement on 16 Apr (WL 41.55 mRL, −0.05 m after 15 Apr reversal). Berm 73 freeboard 2.15 m (crest), but survey shows B73_1 drops to only **1.50 m at 30m width** — potentially ~1.45 m at current WL. **Seepage at berm toe continues.** Pumps at max capacity.
- **Paddock 72** — Stable (Berm 72 freeboard 2.50 m, infill 50.80 mRL on 16 Apr, −0.10 m). Habelo Valley separation berm eroded by rainfall — reinstatement needed.
- **Paddock 61** — Water drying out. Emergency pipes burnt — reinstatement agreed (still pending on 16 Apr).
- **Paddock 16C** — MSP Rejects redirected here on 15 Apr; by 16 Apr discharge accumulation had **reversed the flow direction**, causing backflow and sidewall erosion. Discharge to be reallocated and extended.
- **Paddocks 69, 58/68, 66, 60** — Water level building up, siphoning required (per 15 Apr report; not covered in 16 Apr report)
- **ISOA Paddock** — On the limit (freeboard 2.30 m, WL 27.20 mRL unchanged, berm wall erosion noted, liner reinstatement needed, siphoning required)
- **External Paddock 7** — Water level building up (49.55 mRL unchanged), siphoning required, supernatant removal needed

## Facilities — Tails Stackers

- **Tails 1A** — Back of WCPA Pond / starboard sidewall (safe to operate, 16 Apr)
- **Tails 1B** — Port sidewall WCPA. Stack point +4 m above safe, base +2.5 m, berm height +1.5 m, roping at U/F, O/F eroding berm. **13–15 Apr reports marked NOT safe; 16 Apr report marks "Safe = Yes" despite unchanged geometry — data quality concern** ([[Daily Geotechnical Report — 16 April 2026#Data Quality Notes|see notes]]).
- **Tails 2A** — Berm 75 (NOT safe on 15 Apr at +2 m; 16 Apr report does not include a standalone 2A berm assessment)
- **Tails 2A O/E** — Paddock 72 (safe to operate, HME access flooded — no safe access)
- **Tails 3A** — TSF stockpile. **Fresh washout on 16 Apr** — operations halted. Retention berm at relocated position both narrow and short, access road along base poses HME bogging risk.
- **Tails 3B** — TSF stockpile (photo-only record on 15–16 Apr)
- **BB1** — Washout on 15 Apr, wet tailings toward DMC pit. Retention berm to be constructed. O/F hose leak repaired.

## Valleys

- **Valley 3 Embankment** — disturbed during infrastructure removal, further disturbed by rainfall. Reinstatement in progress.
- **Namalope West Valley 1 (upstream)** — water level 25.70 mRL, approaching/exceeding initial allowable

## Active Site-Wide Issues (16 Apr 2026)

- **WCPC RED ALERT — deteriorating** — freeboard dropped to 1.10 m on 16 Apr (was 1.15 m for five days). Sixth consecutive day in Red. Non-compliant. Godwin pump running, all inflows suspended.
- **MSP Rejects cascade** — Old DM Pit entered Orange on 15 Apr (20.70→21.20 mRL) and MSP Rejects were redirected to Paddock 16C. By 16 Apr, P16C accumulation had **reversed flow direction**, causing backflow and sidewall erosion. Discharge requires further reallocation.
- **Active stacker washouts** — Tails 3A (fresh washout 16 Apr, relocated stacker's new retention berm inadequate); BB1 (washout 15 Apr, wet tailings toward DMC pit).
- **WCPA Pond stabilised** — WL steady at 38.00 mRL (15–16 Apr). No longer at Red Alert threshold.
- **Liner theft** — relentless. Port sidewalls of PDP-1 and PDP-2 liners ripped off again on 16 Apr; another TSF starter wall liner section stolen on 16 Apr. Freshly reinstated liners ripped off repeatedly. Exposed sections eroding during rainy season.
- **Rainfall-induced erosion** — widespread across access roads, berms, Habelo Valley separation berm, Valley 3 embankment, and sidewalls. PDP-2 starboard erosion near slimes boosters worsening and adjacent to Berm 75 worsening. Pipeline leaks from PDP-1/Berm 75 pose dual risk of embankment breach and pipeline integrity loss.
- **TSF pool rising again** — 24.30→24.50 mRL on 16 Apr morning (+0.20 m) after 67,492 m³ slurry receipt on 15 Apr (5× the 14 Apr volume). Freeboard 5.50 m (Yellow TARP).
- **TSF capacity model revised** — available storage raised from 741,000 t to 1,200,000 t on 14 Apr; 58% filled on 15 Apr (696,764 t). Bathymetric survey urgently needed to verify.
- **Paddock 73 seepage persists** — WL slight improvement on 16 Apr (41.55, −0.05 m) after 15 Apr reversal. Seepage at Berm 73 toe continues.
- **SMO berm breach risk** — tailings encroaching on separation berm with slumps developing (per 15 Apr; not covered in 16 Apr report).
- **Tails 1B safety flag inconsistency** — marked "Safe" on 16 Apr despite unchanged +4 m variance, roping, and O/F erosion. Contradicts 13–15 Apr "NOT safe" flags.
- **[[PDP/TSF Automation Project]]** — 12-action programme to centralise SCADA monitoring and automate pump control. 5 of 12 actions complete. Critical path: flow meter and TSF comms interface procurement (long lead times to June 2026).
- **Float-off Pond** — WL 9.10 mRL (stable for 14–16 Apr; earlier "9.80" previous-day figures traced to a copy-paste error). Erosion at entrance from rainfall; discharge to be extended.
