# Lift 1 Construction Log

Compiled from the "Lift 1 construction TSF" WhatsApp group chat (15 Dec 2025 – 09 Apr 2026). This group coordinates the earthworks raise of [[Nataka TSF]] Lift 1, which extends TSF capacity beyond Phase 1.

## Overview

Lift 1 construction involves hauling stacker sand from a stockpile to the TSF wall, spreading it in 750 mm layers, compacting with a roller, and verifying with DCP and Troxler density tests before advancing to the next layer. Work runs 24/7 on day/night shifts (Teams A–D).

## Production

Typical daily production: **100–200 ADT loads/day** across day and night shifts (3–6 ADTs operating).

Notable production records from shift summaries:
- **Dec 2025:** 89–148 loads/day with 2–3 ADTs
- **Jan 2026:** 76–140 loads/day
- **Feb 2026:** 109–200+ loads/day with 3–4 ADTs; most productive period
- **Mar 2026:** 110–180 loads/day; access disruptions from rain; Lalgy ADTs added
- **Apr 2026:** Production reduced — ADTs diverted to red sand haulage, PDP access, engineering tasks

## Construction Method

1. **Survey** — Pegs installed at target RL to guide dumping
2. **Haul** — ADTs loaded by FEL/excavator at stacker stockpile, tip at designated RL
3. **Spread** — Bulldozer or dozer spreads sand to 750 mm layer thickness
4. **Compact** — Roller makes 8+ passes
5. **Test** — DCP (Dynamic Cone Penetrometer) and Troxler (nuclear density gauge) tests conducted; results shared in group for QA approval
6. **Advance** — Once tests pass, next 750 mm layer begins

Sand replacement tests and CPTu (Cone Penetration Test with pore pressure) testing are conducted periodically for additional verification.

## Recurring Issues

### Roller Availability (Critical Path)
The roller has been the persistent bottleneck. KME2509 was on breakdown for most of the project. Substitute rollers (R21SA from TPH, C030) were brought in but C030 also broke down (turbo issues) from late March. Without the roller, compaction cannot proceed, dumping space fills up, and the entire operation stalls.

### Operator Shortages
ADTs frequently stood idle due to missing operators (leave, illness, access gate issues for subcontractor staff). Typical availability was 3 out of 4–6 ADTs.

### Access Road Conditions
Heavy rain regularly made the TSF access road impassable. ADTs got stuck, operations halted while dozers were called from Shore Services to fix access. This was a recurring problem from December through April.

### Dozer Availability
Bulldozers were in constant demand for spreading but frequently diverted to Shore Services, WCP-A walkway moves, or MCC (Mining Conveyor) pulling. The TSF team regularly requested dozer release from other departments.

### Machine Breakdowns & Fuel
- FEL fuel gauges broken (KME2215)
- Excavator fire extinguisher issues (KME2510, Apr)
- Frequent refuelling delays (single bowser shared across site)

### Space Constraints (Apr 2026)
By April, compaction delays caused dumping space to fill up. ADTs were on standby waiting for the roller to free space. Michael Boswell flagged the gap in testwork.

### Non-Conformance & Box Cut
A non-conformance was identified on the south side. Dewatering with a Godwin pump was required before the box cut could be excavated. Still pending as of 09 Apr 2026.
