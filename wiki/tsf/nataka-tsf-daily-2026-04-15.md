## Critical Observations

### Slurry Surge — First Major Deposition Since Plant Restart
Slimes received today: **67,492 m³** — a **5× jump** from the 13,049 m³ received on 14 Apr (the first deposition after the plant shutdown). Average slurry density 1.15 t/m³. Solids received today ≈ 16,150 t.

The chart of daily deposition (Section 3 of the report) shows zero slimes from 09–13 Apr, a modest bar on 14 Apr (~2,000 dry tonnes), and a **sharp spike to ~16,000 dry tonnes on 15 Apr**. The density trace collapses to near zero during the shutdown and recovers to ~1.15 t/m³ on 14–15 Apr.

### Pool Rising Again — but Report is Internally Inconsistent
After stabilising at 24.30 mRL for 14 Apr, the 15 Apr report gives **three different pool elevation values on the same day** (see [[#Data Quality Notes]]). The consistent reading with the reported 5.50 m freeboard (crest 30.00 mRL) is **24.50 mRL** (Op Notes). The 16 Apr morning geotech reading then shows TSF WL at 24.50 mRL, confirming the Op-Notes value is the one that was carried forward. Taking 24.50 mRL as the operative figure: pool up +0.20 m, consistent with the 67,492 m³ slurry input vs 20,219 m³ return outflow.

### Water Return Continues to Strengthen
Water returned TSF → Pond: **20,219 m³/d** — up from 15,593 on 14 Apr and 3,638 on 13 Apr. All four pumps (Supernatant 1 & 2, EDP 1, EDP 2) at 100% availability. Return rate is now about 30% of the day's slurry input.

### Lift 1 Construction Remains Stalled
- Fill to date: 245,230 m³ (unchanged — zero new fill)
- 7-day average fill: 0 m³
- Estimated completion slipped by one more day to **24 May 2026**
- ADTs on 15 Apr were loading sand to improve access (not placing fill)
- TPH lab services on site; QC equipment for wall lift construction identified, procurement ongoing
- Runoff accumulating between Valley 3 and TSF toe berms — siphoning to proceed (agreed with EoR)

### 3A Stacker and Starter Wall Issues Persist
- Prolonged 3A stacker washouts continue encroaching on Lift 1, with liquefied tailings eroding the compacted sidewall and flooding the access road
- Starter wall liner theft re-exposing sections to rainfall erosion — reinstatement urgently required
- Note: a **fresh 3A washout** was recorded the next morning (16 Apr) — see [[Daily Geotechnical Report — 16 April 2026]]

## Data Quality Notes

1. **Three pool elevations in the same report:**
   - Summary table (Section 1): implied **24.35 mRL** (from "minimum weekly freeboard 5.7 m based on pool elevation @24.35 mRL" — actually this matches the 14 Apr reading, so it is effectively last-week's minimum-FB reading, not today's)
   - Operational Notes (Section 2): "**Pool level: 24.50 mRL**"
   - Pool, Beach & Freeboard table (Section 4): "Pool elevation 24.70 mRL"
   - If pool is 24.70 mRL, freeboard = 30.00 − 24.70 = 5.30 m, but reported 5.50 m
   - If pool is 24.50 mRL, freeboard = 30.00 − 24.50 = 5.50 m ✓
   - Most likely: **24.50 mRL** is the operative value; **24.70 mRL** in the table is a transcription error
2. **"Supernatant Pump 1" listed twice** in the Return Water table (first and second rows both show "Supernatant Pump 1 Availability"). The second row is almost certainly meant to read "Supernatant Pump 2 Availability" — consistent with prior reports and with both Supernatants being listed in the infrastructure inventory.
3. **Cross-report pool discrepancy:** The 15 Apr geotech report (morning walk) recorded TSF WL at 24.30 mRL, whereas this TSF daily summary records 24.50–24.70 mRL. The 16 Apr geotech "previous day" field is 24.30 mRL (consistent with the 15 Apr geotech reading). The TSF daily summary pool is sampled later in the day than the geotech inspection — the 0.20 m intra-day rise is consistent with 67,492 m³ slurry being received. The 24.70 mRL figure, if real, would imply a brief peak that then dropped back to 24.50 mRL overnight before the 16 Apr morning inspection.
