## 8. TSF Lift 1 Construction — Issues & Corrective Actions

### Active Issues (CRITICAL)

1. **Loading and tipping operations suspended** — HMEs reestablishing access roads after intensive rainfall.
2. **Runoff accumulating** between Valley 3 and the TSF toe berms — water must be siphoned away and monitoring continued as agreed with the EoR.
3. **Liner reinstatement required** — cut section of liner showing erosion from recent rainfall.
4. **Lift 1 wall erosion** — rainfall-induced erosion observed, reinstatement required.
5. **3A stacker washouts** — wet tailings encroaching on Lift 1 construction, eroding the sidewall.

### Risks from Stacker Washouts

- **Construction Progress Impact:** Uncontrolled flow has flooded the access road, restricting HME movement.
- **Structural Integrity Risk:** Wet tailings have reached and eroded the compacted wall area of Lift 1, potentially undermining TSF stability.
- **Equipment Safety Risk:** Liquefied sand tailings pose a risk of immobilizing HMEs.

### Corrective Measures (Field Meeting Outcomes)

- **Stop and Relocate Stacker** — done
- **Construct Retention Berms** — temporary washout berms at new stacker position to protect drains
- **Maintain Proper Density** — ensure stacker does not operate at low density (plant currently on shut)
- **Remediate Affected Areas** — remove wet tailings, recompact, retest impacted sections
- **Maintain Safe Distances** — adequate separation between stackers and hauling points
- **Raise Awareness** — team briefing on operating in liquefied sand tailings
- **Restrict Road Access** — prohibit HME movement near operating stacker, redirect haulage

### Liner/Starter Wall Erosion

Rainfall-induced erosion observed on an exposed section of the TSF starter wall where the liner was previously removed/stolen. Surface erosion and localized material loss may progressively compromise wall integrity and long-term stability if unaddressed. **Urgent rehabilitation and liner reinstatement recommended.**
