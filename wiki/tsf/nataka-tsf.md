# Nataka TSF

Tailings Storage Facility at [[Kenmare Moma Mining (Mauritius) Limited]]'s Moma Mine in Mozambique. The facility stores mineral processing tailings (slimes) from heavy mineral sands operations.

## Current Status (as of 09 Apr 2026)

- **Phase 1** capacity: 741,000 t (design volume)
- **Solids stored to date:** 678,321 t — **92% filled**
- **Remaining capacity:** 62,679 t (~57 days at current deposition rate)
- **Phase 1 capacity date:** 05 June 2026
- **Pool elevation:** 24.60 mRL (dropping — down 0.15 m from 08 Apr, pumps restored)
- **[[Freeboard]]:** 5.40 m (YELLOW TARP, recovering)

## Lift 1 Construction

The Lift 1 raise is underway to extend TSF capacity beyond Phase 1:

- **Fill target:** 325,000 m³
- **Fill to date:** 245,230 m³ (75% complete)
- **Remaining:** 79,770 m³
- **Est. completion:** 16 May 2026 (slipped from 15 May)
- **Current working level:** 33 RL (south side, chainage 0+100 to 0+175, as of 09 Apr 2026)
- **Method:** 750 mm sand layers, compacted (8+ roller passes), verified by DCP and Troxler tests

**Construction is fully suspended** — zero fill for at least two consecutive reporting periods as of 09 Apr. HMEs focused on reestablishing access roads after 200 mm rainfall on 02 Apr. Tails 3A stacker washouts have encroached on Lift 1, eroding the sidewall (stacker relocated following field meeting). Roller breakdowns (C030 turbo, KME2509 long-term) and ADT diversions compound delays. Capacity margin narrowed to 20 days (16 May completion vs 05 June Phase 1 capacity). See [[Lift 1 Construction Log]], [[Nataka TSF Daily Summary Report — 09 April 2026]], and [[Nataka TSF Weekly Report — W15 2026]].

## Infrastructure

- **Supernatant pumps:** 2x — both at 100% availability as of 09 Apr (restored from priming faults). When running, Supernatant 1 delivers ~1,500 m³/hr.
- **Slimes extraction pumps (EDPs):** 4x (portside PS6030, starboard PS6031, centre PS0543, 4th pump). EDP 1 and EDP 2 at 100% availability as of 09 Apr (restored from high-WL shutdown). Typical flow 400–550 m³/hr each at 1.12–1.28 SGU.
- **Electrical pumps:** 2x (previously both down — V-belt failures)
- **Decanting pumps:** 2x — access to Pump No. 2 being created (red sand haulage for access road)
- **Talweg pump:** Routes water to PDP; stopped when PDP WL is high
- **Flocculant plant:** Down (Main Plant A shutdown; chronic silo issues)
- **PDP return water pumps:** 4x (6040–6043) — manage pond water level at ~31.7 mRL
- **Groundwater drains (GWD):** Currently inaccessible (talweg safety)
- **Sand drains:** Currently inaccessible (talweg safety)

See [[TSF Operations Log]] for detailed pump status history.

## Key Concerns

1. **Capacity timeline is critical** — only 20-day margin between Lift 1 completion (16 May) and Phase 1 capacity (05 June), with zero construction progress for two consecutive periods
2. **Freeboard recovering but historically depressed** — first day-on-day gain (5.25 → 5.40 m) after weeks of decline from ~7.0 m. Still Yellow TARP.
3. **Bathymetric survey overdue** — pool volume monitoring suspended; QPO review blocked
4. **Liner integrity** — multiple sections removed/stolen, erosion observed at starter wall. Theft pattern continuing — another section stolen confirmed 10 Apr geotech inspection despite prior reinstatements.
5. **Seepage at Lift 2 toe** — observed 08 Apr 2026, close monitoring required
6. **Pipeline hanging** — slimes pipeline on starter wall bend hanging into TSF, progressive collapse risk
7. **Drainage access** — GWD and sand drains inaccessible due to talweg conditions
8. **Water return restored but fragile (09 Apr)** — 16,249 m³/d now flowing TSF → Pond after all pumps were down on 08 Apr. Pool level dropping (24.60 mRL, down from 24.75). TSF daily balance flipped to -17,986 m³/d (net outflow). Remains above max allowable 24.0 mRL.
9. **Lift 1 roller bottleneck** — KME2509 on long-term breakdown, C030 turbo failure from late Mar. Compaction delays causing dumping space constraints.
10. **3A stacker washouts damaging Lift 1** — wet tailings eroding compacted wall, flooding access road, immobilizing HMEs. Stacker relocated, retention berms under construction.
11. **SMO berm breach risk** — tailings encroaching on separation berm, slumps developing. Sea wall erosion from stacker washouts.

## Water Balance (WCPA System)

The TSF sits within the broader WCPA (Water Containment and Processing Area) system:

- **PDP daily balance:** -3,423 m³/d
- **WCPA daily balance:** +29,881 m³/d
