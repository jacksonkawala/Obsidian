# Nataka TSF

Tailings Storage Facility at [[Kenmare Moma Mining (Mauritius) Limited]]'s Moma Mine in Mozambique. The facility stores mineral processing tailings (slimes) from heavy mineral sands operations.

## Current Status (as of 16 Apr 2026)

- **Phase 1** capacity: 741,000 t (original design volume)
- **Revised capacity (unverified):** 1,200,000 t — based on assumed dry density and calibrated models; bathymetric survey required to confirm
- **Solids stored to date:** 696,764 t (+16,150 t on 15 Apr) — **58% filled** (revised model)
- **Remaining capacity:** 503,236 t / 96 days (revised, per 15 Apr report)
- **Phase 1 capacity date:** 20 July 2026 (revised, unchanged)
- **Pool elevation:** 24.50 mRL (16 Apr morning, +0.20 m since 15 Apr morning)
- **[[Freeboard]]:** 5.50 m (YELLOW TARP)
- **Plant fully online** — 67,492 m³ slurry received on 15 Apr (5× the 14 Apr volume); 13,049 m³ on 14 Apr

## Lift 1 Construction

The Lift 1 raise is underway to extend TSF capacity beyond Phase 1:

- **Fill target:** 325,000 m³
- **Fill to date:** 245,230 m³ (75% complete)
- **Remaining:** 79,770 m³
- **Est. completion:** 24 May 2026 (slipped from 23 May on 14 Apr, 21 May on 13 Apr, 19 May on 12 Apr)
- **Current working level:** 33 RL (south side, chainage 0+100 to 0+175)
- **Method:** 750 mm sand layers, compacted (8+ roller passes), verified by DCP and Troxler tests

**Construction has effectively stalled** — zero fill placed on 13, 14, and 15 Apr; the 7-day average remains at 0 m³. ADTs were loading sand to improve access on 15 Apr (not placing fill). Tails 3A stacker washouts continue encroaching on Lift 1, eroding the compacted sidewall and flooding the access road. Stacker relocated (done); a fresh 3A washout occurred on 16 Apr, revealing that the retention berm at the new position is both narrow and short with an access road running along its base. Affected areas must be stripped, recompacted, and retested. Capacity margin is 57 days under the revised model (24 May completion vs 20 Jul capacity). TPH lab services on site. QC equipment for wall lift construction identified and procurement ongoing. See [[Lift 1 Construction Log]], [[Nataka TSF Daily Summary Report — 15 April 2026]], and [[Daily Geotechnical Report — 16 April 2026]].

## Infrastructure

- **Supernatant pumps:** 2x — both at 100% availability as of 09 Apr (restored from priming faults). When running, Supernatant 1 delivers ~1,500 m³/hr.
- **Slimes extraction pumps (EDPs):** 4x (portside PS6030, starboard PS6031, centre PS0543, 4th pump). EDP 1 and EDP 2 at 100% availability as of 09 Apr (restored from high-WL shutdown). Typical flow 400–550 m³/hr each at 1.12–1.28 SGU.
- **Electrical pumps:** 2x (previously both down — V-belt failures)
- **Decanting pumps:** 2x — access to Pump No. 2 being created (red sand haulage for access road)
- **Talweg pump:** Routes water to PDP; stopped when PDP WL is high
- **Flocculant plant:** Down (Main Plant A shutdown; chronic silo issues)
- **PDP return water pumps:** 4x (6040–6043) — manage pond water level at ~31.7 mRL
- **Groundwater drains (GWD):** Currently inaccessible (talweg safety)
- **Sand drains:** Currently inaccessible (talweg safety)

See [[TSF Operations Log]] for detailed pump status history.

## Key Concerns

1. **Capacity model revised but unverified** — the 14 Apr TSF report raised available capacity from 741,000 t to 1,200,000 t, extending the deadline from 05 Jun to 20 Jul 2026. However, this is based on assumed dry density and calibrated models; a bathymetric survey is urgently required to confirm. Under the original model, only ~35 days remain (vs 96 under the revised).
2. **Freeboard sliding again** — after recovering to 6.65 m (12 Apr), freeboard dropped to 5.65 m (13 Apr) on pool rise, stabilised at 5.70 m (14 Apr), dropped to 5.50 m on 15 Apr as slurry receipt resumed at 5× volume. Still Yellow TARP.
3. **Bathymetric survey overdue** — pool volume monitoring suspended; QPO review blocked
4. **Liner integrity** — theft continues relentlessly. Liner sections stolen on TSF starter wall, PDP-1 and PDP-2 port sidewalls on multiple occasions. Another starter wall liner section stolen on 16 Apr. Exposed sections eroding during rainy season.
5. **Seepage at Lift 2 toe** — observed 08 Apr 2026, still reported on 16 Apr; monitoring continues
6. **Pipeline hanging** — slimes pipeline on starter wall bend hanging into TSF, progressive collapse risk; repositioning and securing recommended
7. **Drainage access** — GWD and sand drains inaccessible due to talweg conditions
8. **Water balance flipped positive on 15 Apr** — TSF balance +43,385 m³/d on 15 Apr (driven by 67,492 m³ slurry receipt vs 20,219 m³ return). WCPA pond balance moderated to +30,915. TSF pool rising again as a result (24.30→24.50 mRL on 16 Apr morning).
9. **Lift 1 roller bottleneck** — KME2509 on long-term breakdown, C030 turbo failure from late Mar. Compaction delays causing dumping space constraints.
10. **Active stacker washouts** — Tails 3A (16 Apr fresh washout, relocated stacker's new retention berm inadequate); BB1 (15 Apr washout, wet tailings toward DMC pit). Affected areas require stripping, recompaction, retesting. Wet tailings reached compacted wall area and flooded access road.
11. **WCPC still RED** — freeboard now **1.10 m** on 16 Apr (sixth consecutive day in Red; down from 1.15 m held for five straight). Non-compliant. Godwin pump running, inflows suspended.
12. **MSP Rejects cascade** — Old DM Pit ORANGE on 15 Apr triggered redirection to Paddock 16C; by 16 Apr, P16C accumulation reversed flow direction causing backflow and sidewall erosion. Further reallocation required.
13. **Paddock 73 seepage** — seepage at Berm 73 toe continues (14–16 Apr). WL reversed briefly on 15 Apr (41.30→41.60) then slight improvement on 16 Apr (41.55).

## Water Balance (WCPA System, 15 Apr)

The TSF sits within the broader WCPA (Water Containment and Processing Area) system:

- **TSF daily balance:** +43,385 m³/d (inflow 67,492 from PDP; outflows 20,219 to pond + 3,888 seepage)
- **PDP daily balance:** -60,764 m³/d (inflow 90,000 from WCP-A; outflows 67,492 to TSF + 82,000 to pond + 1,272 seepage)
- **WCPA daily balance:** +30,915 m³/d (major inflows from PDP 82,000 + stacker R/W 43,017 + RAW Dam 18,340 + Finishing Pond C 16,800 + TSF return 20,219; outflows to PDP 90,000 + Tails 53,771 + cons 2,523 + seepage 3,168)
