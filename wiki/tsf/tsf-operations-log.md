# TSF Operations Log

Compiled from the "TSF Operations" WhatsApp group chat (08 Oct 2025 – 09 Apr 2026). This group coordinates day-to-day water management at the [[Nataka TSF]] and PDP system, including slimes extraction, supernatant return, and water level monitoring.

## Overview

The TSF Operations group provides shift-by-shift status updates (roughly every 2 hours) on pump availability, flow rates, slimes densities, and water levels across the WCPA system. The primary operational cycle is:

1. **Slimes extraction** — Pumps at PDP extract settled slimes and pump them to TSF for permanent storage
2. **Supernatant return** — TSF water (supernatant) is pumped back to the pond for reuse
3. **Talweg management** — Talweg water is routed to PDP; flow rates managed to control water balance
4. **Water level monitoring** — TSF WL, PDP WL, and Pond WL tracked against maximum allowable limits

## Operational Timeline

### Oct–Nov 2025: Baseline Operations
- Group started 08 Oct 2025. Jackson added 10 Nov 2025.
- Two extraction pumps (portside + starboard) running; typical combined flow ~1,000 m³/hr.
- Stacker repositioning coordinated (T3 move, 10 Nov).
- PDP WL managed at ~33.4–33.9 mRL; PWL compliance at 31.8 mRL.

- Return water pumps (6040–6043) toggled to maintain PWL; typically 3 pumps at 65%.

### Dec 2025 – Jan 2026: Steady State
- Operations routine; regular 2-hourly pump status updates.
- Supernatant pump running at ~1,300–1,500 m³/hr returning water to pond.
- TSF WL around 19.8–21.9 mRL (well below max).

### Feb 2026: Plant Shutdowns & Water Stress
- WCPA plant off-feed periods — all extraction pumps stopped during shutdowns.
- Supernatant 2 frequently down; relying on Supernatant 1 alone (~1,500 m³/hr).
- PDP WL rising to 34.0 mRL (approaching max 34.2 mRL).
- Pond WL at 35.0–35.75 mRL (approaching max 36.0 mRL).
- Flocculant plant down (silo level issues, reading device malfunction).

### Mar 2026: Escalating Water Issues
- **All systems down** for extended period (~15 Mar): every pump marked red, no water return.
- TSF WL reaching 23.8 mRL (0.2 m from max allowable).
- PDP WL at 34.6–34.8 mRL (**exceeding max** 34.2 mRL).
- Pond WL at 36.2 mRL (**exceeding max** 36.0 mRL).
- Slimes extraction booster eroded area requiring dozer repair.
- Decanting pump inspections; discharge line reallocation for EDP3.
- Winch rope clamp failures — no replacement clamps in stock.

### Apr 2026: Crisis — All Pumps Down, TSF Overtopping Risk
- **TSF WL at 24.6 mRL** — vertical marker submerged, water approaching supernatant pump platforms.
- **All 4 extraction pumps down** due to high TSF water level (can't pump more slimes in).
- **All 4 return water pumps down** — Supernatant 1 & 2 priming pump faults; Electrical Pump 1 V-belts snapped; Electrical Pump 2 down.
- **Flocculant plant shut down** (Main Plant A scheduled shutdown).
- Emergency tasks assigned: service supernatant priming pumps (top priority), replace V-belts, install submersible pump, create access to decanting pump No. 2.
- Portside extraction pump showing density 1.19 SGU but zero flow — all pumps stopped.
- Some Portuguese-language reports from operators (Beto António) describing pump pontoon conditions.

## Recurring Issues

### Supernatant Pump Reliability
Supernatant 2 was down for most of the observation period. When Supernatant 1 also failed (priming pump fault, Apr 2026), there was zero water return capacity from TSF — directly causing the water level crisis.

### PDP Water Level Non-Compliance
PDP WL regularly exceeded the 34.2 mRL max allowable, sometimes reaching 34.8 mRL. This limited the ability to run the talweg pump and created cascading constraints.

### Flocculant Plant
Chronic issues: silo level reading faults, acquisition/supply problems, rubber seal damage. Plant was more often down than running.

### Winch Clamp Failures
Extraction pump winch clamps repeatedly damaged. New setpoints needed to prevent recurrence, but replacement clamps were not in stock.

### Communication Gaps
Pump status reports sometimes posted hours late, readings missing ("Flow: m³/hr" with no value), or night shift reports duplicated/corrected the next morning.
