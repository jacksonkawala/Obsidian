## Overview

The mine received a total of **1,748,917 m³** of make-up water in March 2026, an increase of **653,027 m³** (+59%) compared to February. The increase is largely driven by a 156% surge in borefield abstraction, reflecting the dry-season ramp-up and the commissioning of the PDP system.

| Source | Volume (m³) | Share |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Borefield | 1,265,941 | 72.4% |
| Mualadi River | 470,742 | 26.9% |
| Mavele Lake | 12,234 | 0.7% |
| **Total** | **1,748,917** | |

Water withdrawals from the Mualadi River and Lake Mavele fell by 12% and 81% respectively, while borefield abstraction increased 156% month-on-month.

## Water Reuse

Process water recirculated in March totalled **15,831,202 m³** (averaging 21,196 m³/h), yielding a water reuse percentage of **86.05%** (year-to-date: **86.92%**). The reuse calculation was adjusted in March to account for the WCPA upgrade, PDP system commissioning, and TSF commissioning. January and February reuse figures were retroactively amended to 87.58% and 87.29%.

Key recirculation streams include:
- Calen dredge process water: 3,211,562 m³
- Sandra dredge process water: 2,957,108 m³
- PDP-1 to WCPA Pond: 2,590,756 m³
- Deidre process water: 2,302,027 m³
- Julia process water: 951,177 m³
- TSF return pump: 749,781 m³
- WCPC Finishing Pond to WCPA Pond: 499,800 m³
- MSP rejects to old WCPC paddocks: 618,188 m³

## Water Balance Highlights

The operational water balance shows substantial month-on-month changes driven by the PDP system coming online and the wet-to-dry season transition:

| Parameter | Feb (m³/h) | Mar (m³/h) | Variance |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Boreholes to RWD | 735.86 | 1,701.53 | +965.67 |
| Raw Water to WCPA | 455.27 | 913.47 | +458.20 |
| WCPA Seepage & Storage | 665.27 | 2,443.93 | +1,778.66 |
| TSF return pump | 384.86 | 1,007.77 | +622.91 |
| PDP to TSF | 139.69 | 984.54 | +844.85 |
| Namalope Rainfall (mm) | 3.00 | 404.00 | +401.00 |
| Pilivili Rainfall (mm) | 28.00 | 124.00 | +96.00 |

Notable: March was an exceptionally wet month — Namalope received **404 mm** of rainfall (up from 3 mm in February). This heavy rainfall contributed to site-wide water management challenges and elevated storage volumes across WCPA, WCPB, and WCPC systems.

The PDP system is now fully operational, with new flow paths (WCPA to PDP at 5,223 m³/h, PDP to WCPA at 3,482 m³/h) and associated seepage/storage and evaporation losses recorded for the first time.
